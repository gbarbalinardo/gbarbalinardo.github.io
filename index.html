Giuseppe Barbalinardo.

Developer, Scientist, and Inventor.

Ciao,

I am Giuseppe Barbalinardo, a computational scientist and a software developer. I am part of the Nanotheory Research Group at the University of California, Davis. During my PhD, I have worked on the development of kALDo, an open source project to simulate heat at the nanoscale. I am currently a NSF MOLSSI Investment Software Fellow, and the 2020 recipient of the Peter A. Rock Graduate Fellowship in Physical Chemistry.

I am interested in the overlapping areas between software development and mathematical modeling, and passionate about Natural Language Processing. Before joining my current research group, I worked as a software developer and iOS engineering manager at Grio in San Francisco.

Portfolio

Heat is a mysterious quantity that has puzzled scientists for centuries, and its understanding is today more important than ever. We can simulate heat transport at the nanoscale using numerical models, at its fundamental atomic level. kALDo is a modern Python-based software that implements both the Boltzmann Transport equation and the Quasi-Harmonic Green Kubo method, which runs on GPUs and CPUs using Tensorflow. It is released open-source, for the scientific community to use and develop.
